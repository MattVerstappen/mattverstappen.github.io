## About

This paper examines Cory Barlog, Creative Director of Sony's Santa Monica Studio, as a case study in contemporary design leadership, analysing how his direction of God of War (2018) represents a landmark example of visionary, transformational, and reflexive leadership in AAA game development.

### Key Leadership Themes Analysed

**Personal experience as creative foundation:** Barlog's experiences as a new father directly shaped the emotional core of God of War (2018), particularly the Kratos-Atreus father-son relationship, demonstrating how personal narrative can drive meaningful game design.

**Iterative precision and developmental transparency:** Barlog's commitment to iteration and openness about the development process - including his emotional investment in the project - fostered psychological safety and creative empowerment across the studio.

**Servant-leadership principles:** The paper analyses how Barlog balanced creative autonomy with operational discipline, using servant-leadership frameworks to empower his team rather than direct them.

**Interdisciplinary collaboration:** Nordic cultural research trips and cross-disciplinary collaboration helped the studio authentically integrate Norse mythology into the game's world-building.

**Technical innovation:** The one-shot camera technique - maintaining a single unbroken camera take throughout the entire game - is analysed as both a creative and operational leadership decision that redefined AAA narrative benchmarks.

### Leadership Frameworks Applied

- Transformational Leadership
- Visionary Leadership
- Servant-Leadership
- Reflexive Design Leadership

### Part Two: Fictional Recruitment Brief

The second part of the paper presents a fictional executive recruitment brief for a Creative Director of Narrative Innovation at Santa Monica Studio, modelled on the leadership competencies Barlog embodied throughout God of War (2018).
