## About

This paper examines the South African game development landscape and articulates a personal vision for design leadership within it.

### Part One: The South African Game Development Landscape

The South African games industry is dominated by micro-enterprises of one to five employees and shaped by structural tensions between creative innovation and infrastructural constraints:

- Load shedding disrupting development workflows
- High data costs limiting access to tools and collaboration
- Fragmented government support with inconsistent funding programmes

**Geographic imbalance:** 55.9% of studios are concentrated in the Western Cape, leaving other provinces underserved.

**Market projections:** Sub-Saharan gaming markets are projected to reach R20 billion in value by 2025.

### Case Studies

**Free Lives (Cape Town)**
- Model centred on creative autonomy
- Known for: *Broforce*, *Anger Foot*
- Approach: small team, high creative control, international distribution

**Carry1st (Pan-African)**
- Pan-African mobile publisher with over R500 million in funding
- Targeting Sub-Saharan markets with mobile-first titles
- Demonstrating international investor confidence in African game development

### Part Two: A Personal Design Leadership Vision

A personal design leadership vision centred on building a **decentralised network of game development incubators** in township and rural areas across South Africa.

**Key pillars of the vision:**

- **Entrepreneurship** — Equipping emerging developers with business skills alongside technical ones
- **Cultural storytelling** — Centring African narratives, languages, and contexts in games
- **Offline-friendly tools** — Prioritising workflows that function without reliable connectivity
- **Community-driven curriculum** — Curricula co-designed with local communities rather than imposed from outside
- **Geographic redistribution** — Shifting the industry's centre of gravity beyond Cape Town

The vision aims to democratise access to game development education and create a more equitable, diverse, and creatively authentic South African games industry.
