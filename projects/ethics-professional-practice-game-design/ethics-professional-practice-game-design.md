## About

This paper examines ethics and professional practice in game design from a South African perspective, arguing that game designers hold unique moral responsibilities given the medium's capacity to shape player behaviour, emotional states, and cognitive patterns.

### Eight Ethical Domains Surveyed

1. **Monetisation ethics and dark patterns** - Loot boxes, fake currency systems, and exploitative engagement loops; assessment against fair design principles.

2. **Representation and cultural sensitivity** - The urgent need for authentic representation in the South African colonial context, where cultural misrepresentation carries particular historical weight.

3. **Accessibility and inclusion** - Drawing on *The Last of Us Part II* and *Celeste* as industry benchmarks for inclusive design practice.

4. **AI-generated content** - Risks relating to copyright infringement, IP theft, and the displacement of creative professionals.

5. **POPIA compliance and data protection** - South African data protection law and its application to game development studios handling player data.

6. **Crunch culture and workplace exploitation** - Systemic overwork in the games industry and its human cost, with analysis of structural causes.

7. **Environmental sustainability** - The carbon footprint of game development infrastructure, from servers to hardware manufacturing.

### Personal Application

The second part applies these frameworks personally, outlining integration of ethical practices throughout the design process:

- Ethical impact assessments in pre-production
- Fair monetisation models inspired by *Stardew Valley* and *Path of Exile*
- Accessibility features integrated in early prototyping
- Authentically African narratives through diverse hiring and community consultation
