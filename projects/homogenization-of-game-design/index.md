## About

This paper critically examines how the widespread adoption of AI-driven development tools is contributing to a growing homogenisation of game design. The central argument is that while AI technologies such as procedural generation and automated asset creation have improved production efficiency, they have simultaneously reduced the diversity of artistic expression and stifled creative innovation across the industry.

### Three Core Concerns

1. **Aesthetic convergence** — AI-generated content is causing visual aesthetics and mechanical structures to converge into predictable, formulaic patterns across titles and studios.

2. **Loss of creative agency** — Delegating design decisions to AI undermines human creative agency and produces narratives that are structurally coherent but emotionally shallow.

3. **Industry displacement** — Approximately 78% of triple-A studios now rely on AI-based generation techniques, displacing skilled artists, writers, and designers while raising unresolved questions around authorship and intellectual property.

### Conclusion

AI, if left unchecked, threatens both the creative integrity and economic stability of the games industry. The paper calls for future research into frameworks that position AI as an assistive tool rather than a replacement for human creativity.
