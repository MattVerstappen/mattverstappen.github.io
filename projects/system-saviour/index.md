## About

This project proposes System Saviour, a rugged, modular, offline-compatible diagnostic device addressing the critical gap in affordable tech repair tools across South Africa.

### The Problem

- Device replacement costs are prohibitive for most South Africans
- South Africa generated 360,000 tonnes of e-waste in 2024; only 14% was properly recycled
- Township and low-income users lack access to standardised diagnostic tools
- Existing professional-grade diagnostic equipment is unaffordable and inaccessible outside urban centres

### Design Principles

- **Cradle-to-Cradle design** - Lifecycle thinking from manufacture to end-of-life
- **Circular economy frameworks** - Repairability and component reuse built in by design
- **Right-to-repair ethics** - Empowering users and local repair technicians rather than locking repair to authorised centres

### Product Specifications

- Compact handheld unit built from recycled plastic
- Modular adapter ports for a range of device types
- 12-hour battery life
- Thermal sensors for hardware diagnostics
- Multilingual UI: isiZulu, English, and Sesotho
- Full hardware and software diagnostic report generated in under 30 seconds
- Real-time part pricing lookup

### Use Case Testing

Testing conducted across three environments:

1. **Informal repair stall, Khayelitsha** - Significant reduction in diagnosis time and improved customer throughput
2. **School ICT lab** - Faster triage and reduced downtime for student devices
3. **Corporate tech environment** - 70% reduction in diagnosis time compared to existing manual processes
